5. Summary of the matter being reported
● AI-drafted
On 30 May 2026 at 14:42, an outbound email from a fee-earner of the firm containing 14 pages of consultant correspondence and a medical history schedule relating to one client of the firm was sent to an external recipient outside the matter's authorised party register. The recipient was an unrelated individual with a similar surname whose address had been incorrectly cached in the sender's autocomplete. The recipient self-reported at 14:50 and confirmed deletion at 15:03. The firm reports this matter under SRA Code of Conduct for Firms paragraph 3.9 on the basis that special category personal data was disclosed in breach of the firm's confidentiality obligations and SRA guidance indicators 4.3 and 4.5 apply. The ICO Article 33 obligation is being addressed in parallel by the firm's nominated data protection partner.
